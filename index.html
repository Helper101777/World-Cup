How We Play

Rules

🔁
Consecutive Rounds: 2 teams face the same opponent for 2 rounds in a row before rotating.
🧤
Rotating Goalkeeper: The 3rd team not playing in those 2 rounds serves as the goalkeeper.
⚡
First Goal: Whoever scores first in an attacking round earns 1 point.
🛡️
Saves & Blocks: Every save, block, or protection = 1 point. Penalty save = 1 bonus point.
⭐
Save Streak Bonus: Achieve 5 saves in a game and earn an extra +1 bonus point.
🟨
Cards: Red and yellow cards can be issued. Standard football rules apply for fouls and conduct.
🏆
Playoffs (R15 & R16): Winner of R13 qualifies for R15. Winner of R14 advances too. R15 winner faces the loser from R13/14 in the Final.
⚽
Standard Football Rules: All regular football rules apply outside of these custom additions.
The Story So Far

Tournament History

2024
The First Edition — Where It All Began
The very first Family World Cup kicked off with 2 teams going head-to-head. A new tradition was born in the backyard, with friendly rivalry and competitive spirit that would only grow from here.
🏆 Champion: Nabil
2025
The Expansion — 16 Rounds of Dominance
United Phoenix FC (Nabil) and Tiger Winners (Nader) went 16 rounds in a grueling season. United Phoenix dominated the campaign, winning all but 2 rounds — losing R9 and R12 — before storming through the playoffs to claim back-to-back titles.
🏆 Champion: Nabil · United Phoenix FC
2026
The New Era — Three Teams, Eight Rounds
A brand new lineup enters the pitch: Tyrant Lizard FC 🦖, Red Lion FC 🦁, and Tiger Winners 🐯. With a rotating goalkeeper system, bonus save points, and playoff drama across 8 rounds — this is the biggest edition yet. Season starts July 4th.

The Spirit of the Game

About

👨‍👩‍👦‍👦

The Family World Cup is our very own football tradition — played between brothers, built on competition, and kept alive by the love of the game.

Started in 2024 with just 2 players and a simple idea, it has grown into a full tournament with custom rules, rotating goalkeepers, bonus point systems, and a playoff bracket that would make any football association proud.

Every goal, every save, every yellow card is part of the story. This site is where we keep the record straight.
